RANKS

Version 0.6.2 (Alpha) — Official Rules

I. Overview

RANKS is a 2-player abstract strategy game of hidden information, spatial control, and combat. The core tension is commitment under uncertainty. Players build sprawling networks to connect opposite sides of a claustrophobic board while navigating brutal, localized skirmishes that force them to reveal their hidden strengths.

II. Components & Setup

Board: One 7×7 grid board.

Pieces: 24 White Tiles (Player 1) and 24 Black Tiles (Player 2). Each tile features a dot value of 1 through 6 on one side and is blank on the reverse. Each value appears exactly four times per set.

Setup: Each player thoroughly shuffles their 24 tiles face-down to form a personal Supply stack.

III. The Opening: The Pie Rule

To mitigate first-mover advantage, the game begins with the Pie Rule:

  1. Player 1 draws one tile from their Supply, looks at it secretly, and Deploys it face-down on any empty cell.
  2. Player 2 then evaluates the board and chooses one of two options:
    • Swap: Player 2 takes control of the White pieces. They may secretly look at the placed tile’s value. The new Player 2 then takes the next turn.
    • Continue: Player 2 remains Black and takes the next turn.

IV. Turn Structure

At the exact start of your turn, before taking an action, you may secretly peek at any one of your own face-down occupied cells currently on the board. You do not reveal it to your opponent.

Note: If you peek at a stack, you may look at both the top and bottom tiles.

You must then perform exactly one of the three core actions: Deploy, Reinforce, or Strike.

Passing

Passing is not permitted unless your Supply is completely empty. If it is empty, you must Strike if a legal Strike exists. A legal Strike exists if any of your occupied cells is orthogonally adjacent to any enemy-occupied cell. If no legal Strike exists, you pass. Two consecutive passes end the game.

V. The Three Actions

1. Deploy (Expand)

Draw the top tile from your Supply, look at it secretly, and place it face-down on any empty cell on the board.

Scorched Earth (The Ko Rule): You may not Deploy a tile into a cell where your Spearhead was Captured on the immediately preceding turn. You must play elsewhere for at least one turn before that cell cools down.

2. Reinforce (Build)

Draw the top tile from your Supply, look at it secretly, and place it face-down on top of any of your existing single tiles (whether face-up or face-down) to form a stack. The top tile is the most recently placed tile. The maximum stack height is two tiles.

3. Strike (Attack)

When you Strike, choose:

  1. One of your occupied cells as the attacker, and
  2. One orthogonally adjacent enemy-occupied cell as the target.

The striking tile must be flipped face-up to declare the attack. (If initiating a Strike with a stack, flip only the top tile face-up first to declare the attack.)

VI. Combat Resolution

Combat resolves immediately and exactly once per Strike action (no cascading captures).

Captured tiles are permanently removed from the game and placed face-up in a shared, public Graveyard. Players may inspect the Graveyard at any time.

Step 1 — Identify Clusters

Attacking Cluster: The striking cell (the Spearhead) plus any friendly cells directly orthogonally adjacent to it (distance 1).

Defending Cluster: The targeted cell (the Spearhead) plus any enemy cells directly orthogonally adjacent to it (distance 1).

Diagonal connections and cells beyond distance 1 are completely ignored.

Step 2 — Reveal

All tiles in both clusters are immediately flipped face-up. (When a stack is revealed, reveal both tiles.) They remain face-up for the rest of the game.

Step 3 — Sum

Calculate the total dot value of each cluster. If a stack is involved, both tiles in that stack contribute their values.

Step 4 — Resolve Casualties & Routs

  • Clear Winner: The cluster with the lower total sum loses. The losing Spearhead is Captured. If the losing Spearhead is a stack, BOTH tiles are Captured.
  • All other tiles in the losing cluster are Routed: they remain on the board permanently face-up but are otherwise unharmed.
  • Tie: Both Spearheads are Captured. All other tiles in both clusters are Routed.

Step 5 — Check Victory

After combat fully resolves, instantly check for Sudden Death conditions.

VII. Winning the Game

1. Sudden Death — Qualifying Connection

After any action (including after combat resolves), if a player has an unbroken orthogonal chain of friendly tiles touching both Row 1 and Row 7, or both Column 1 and Column 7, that player wins immediately only if the chain is Qualified.

Qualified Chain Requirement (The Threshold):

Compute the chain’s Qualification Score: the sum of dot values of all face-up tiles in that chain. Face-down tiles maintain physical connection but contribute 0. If a stack is in the chain, only face-up tiles in that stack count.

Win Condition: The chain is Qualified if its Qualification Score is 21 or greater.

Simultaneous Qualification: If a single combat resolution results in both players forming a Qualified chain simultaneously, the active player (the attacker) wins.

Routed tiles (face-up) count as normal nodes in a chain and contribute their dot values normally.

2. Sudden Death — Extermination

If a player has zero tiles on the board and zero tiles in their Supply, they lose immediately. Checked after each combat resolution.

3. Mutual Attrition — Scoring

If the game ends by two consecutive passes:

  • Simply sum the dot values of all of your face-up tiles currently on the board. (Face-down tiles count as 0.)
  • The highest total score wins.
  • If tied, the player with the most physical tiles remaining on the board wins.
  • If still tied, the game is a draw.

VIII. Final Clarifications

  • Routed Tiles: Function exactly like normal tiles. They can Strike, provide Support, and be Reinforced. They can never be turned face-down again.
  • Stacks: Count as a single spatial node in a chain for connection purposes.
  • Peeking: A player may look at their own face-down tiles in their Supply at any time.
