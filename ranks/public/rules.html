I. Overview

RANKS is a 2-player abstract strategy game of hidden information, spatial control, and combat. Players build fragile networks of hidden and revealed strength while navigating localized skirmishes that force them to expose their weakest points.

II. Components & Setup

Board: One 7×7 grid board.

Pieces: 24 White Tiles and 24 Black Tiles. Each tile shows a dot value 1–6 on one side and blank on the reverse. Each value appears exactly four times per set.

Setup: Each player shuffles their 24 tiles face-down to form a personal Supply stack.

III. The Opening: The Pie Rule

  1. Player 1 draws one tile, looks at it secretly, and Deploys it face-down on any empty cell.
  2. Player 2 chooses either:
    • Swap: take White and move next.
    • Continue: remain Black and move next.

IV. Turn Structure

At the start of your turn, before taking an action, you may secretly peek at one of your own face-down occupied cells. You do not reveal it to your opponent.

You must then perform exactly one action: Deploy or Strike.

Passing: Passing is not permitted unless your Supply is empty. If it is empty, you must Strike if a legal Strike exists. If no legal Strike exists, you pass. Two consecutive passes end the game.

V. The Actions

1. Deploy (Expand)

Draw the top tile from your Supply, look at it secretly, and place it face-down on any empty cell.

Scorched Earth (Ko Rule): You may not Deploy into the cell where your Spearhead was captured on the immediately preceding turn.

2. Strike (Attack)

Choose one of your occupied cells as the attacker and one orthogonally adjacent enemy-occupied cell as the target. The striking tile is flipped face-up to declare the attack.

VI. Combat Resolution

Combat resolves immediately and exactly once per Strike action.

Captured tiles are permanently removed from the game and placed face-up in a shared public Graveyard.

Step 1 — Identify Clusters

Attacking Cluster: the striking cell plus any friendly cells orthogonally adjacent to it.

Defending Cluster: the targeted cell plus any enemy cells orthogonally adjacent to it.

Diagonal connections and cells beyond distance 1 are ignored.

Step 2 — Reveal

All tiles in both clusters are flipped face-up and remain face-up for the rest of the game.

Step 3 — Sum

Calculate the total dot value of each cluster.

Step 4 — Resolve Casualties & Routs

  • Clear Winner: The lower-sum cluster loses. Its Spearhead is Captured.
  • All other tiles in the losing cluster are Routed: they stay on the board face-up and otherwise function normally.
  • Tie: Both Spearheads are Captured. All other tiles in both clusters are Routed.

Step 5 — Check Victory

After combat fully resolves and captured pieces are removed, instantly check for sudden death conditions.

VII. Winning the Game

1. Sudden Death — Valuable Path

After any action, if a player has a Qualified Path, that player wins immediately.

Path: a single simple orthogonal path through your tiles, without branching and without revisiting any cell.

Qualified Path: a path that satisfies both:

  • Minimum Length: at least 7 cells.
  • Value Threshold: the sum of face-up tile values on that path is at least 21.

Face-down tiles maintain connection but contribute 0.

Simultaneous Qualification: if a single combat resolution qualifies both players’ paths simultaneously, the attacker wins.

2. Sudden Death — Extermination

If a player has zero tiles on the board and zero tiles in their Supply, they lose immediately. This is checked after each combat resolution.

3. Mutual Attrition — Scoring

If the game ends by two consecutive passes, each player sums the dot values of all their face-up tiles still on the board. Face-down tiles count as 0. Higher total wins. If tied, the player with more physical tiles on the board wins; if still tied, the game is a draw.

VIII. Final Clarifications

  • Routed Tiles: Routed tiles function exactly like normal tiles, but can never be turned face-down again.
  • Peeking: A player may look at their own face-down tiles in their Supply at any time.
